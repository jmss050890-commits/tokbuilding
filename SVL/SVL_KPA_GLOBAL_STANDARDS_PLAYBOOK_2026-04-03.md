# SVL-KPA Global Standards Playbook

Timestamp: 2026-04-03 06:00:00 -05:00
Owner: SVL Lab
Mission Anchor: Keep People Alive (KPA)

## 1) Global Standard Definition

SVL-KPA Global Standard means every public product and message must:
- protect people
- reduce harm
- support health and stability
- strengthen family/community outcomes
- avoid reckless or manipulative design
- keep mission-first before growth tactics

## 2) Non-Negotiable Guardrails

1. Mission-first gate:
- No launch goes live unless purpose and safety intent are explicit.

2. Safety gate:
- No feature can encourage harmful behavior, dangerous advice, or panic-style UX.

3. Voice gate:
- Human, calm, purposeful language only.
- Avoid robotic defaults in audio where voice is used.

4. Trust gate:
- Claims require evidence, references, or measurable behavior.
- If unknown, state unknown.

5. Accessibility gate:
- Mobile-first usability required.
- Clear labels, readable contrast, and obvious controls.

## 3) Search Atlas Operating Standard (Weekly)

Monday (Technical + Visibility)
- Run full audit and clear all critical/high issues.
- Validate indexing, canonicals, sitemap, robots, and broken links.
- Review rank movement for priority clusters.

Wednesday (Content + Entity Coverage)
- Publish or refresh at least 2 mission-aligned pages.
- Expand semantic coverage for KPA intent terms.
- Improve internal linking from authority hubs.

Friday (Authority + Conversion)
- Run backlink/outreach sprint.
- Update conversion pages and CTAs.
- Capture weekly KPI report and decisions.

## 4) Global SEO Clusters (SVL-KPA)

Cluster A: Mission Authority
- keep people alive technology
- mission-first AI platform
- safety-first digital systems

Cluster B: Product Intent
- crisis support app
- AI health coaching platform
- emergency safety tools

Cluster C: Family/Community Trust
- family safety technology
- community wellness platform
- prevention-first support systems

Cluster D: Brand Leadership
- Sanders Viopro Labs
- SVL KPA standards
- ethical AI safety framework

## 5) Release Criteria (Before Production)

A release passes only if all are true:
- KPA mission statement visible in user-facing experience.
- Voice and copy pass calm/purposeful review.
- No critical technical SEO issues.
- Core pages have metadata and schema where applicable.
- At least one KPI improvement hypothesis is documented.

## 6) KPI Targets (30/60/90)

30 days:
- +20% optimized indexed pages
- all critical technical issues resolved
- baseline conversions mapped per landing page

60 days:
- top-20 ranking movement on 30% of priority non-brand terms
- +30% organic sessions to mission/product pages

90 days:
- stable top-10 positions for priority terms
- measurable conversion lift from SEO-origin traffic

## 7) Governance Rhythm

Weekly:
- 30-minute standards review
- 30-minute Search Atlas execution review
- 15-minute risk/compliance review

Monthly:
- standards scorecard review
- backlog reprioritization
- publish updated SVL progress log

## 8) Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately if:
- critical SEO issues persist > 7 days
- production messaging drifts from KPA mission
- voice behavior regresses to robotic/default patterns
- major ranking or traffic drops exceed 25% week-over-week

## 9) Decision Rule

If growth tactic conflicts with KPA standards:
- reject tactic
- redesign for mission alignment
- ship only after mission + quality gates pass
